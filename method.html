To get the ball rolling, of this Dada poem assignment I chose “Polarize” song
in “blurry face” album of Twenty one pilots.

I believed, one of the song’s messages is that acceptance of the truth
about one’s self will enable a person to know the truth
of what must be done outside the self in the rest of the world.
Personally, I feel a resemblance between this song and Dada movement that
they are both have a kind of rebelious, brave, artitstic, self-questioning,…feeling.
Therefore, my main idea was to visualized this song with dadaist poem technique
based on the lyrics and imagination.
For instance, the main focus of the layout is a big black doors with stairs based on the sentence:
“Those stairs is where I'll be hiding all my problems”
as I imagined a view form the basement bottom up.
Those rotating red and blue patterns represented a hypotized feeling
like a vortex of thinking and finding the true self.
There are lots of hand images are poitning everywhere based on these sentences:
” Polarize is taking your disguises
Separating em, splitting em up from wrong and right
It's deciding where to die and deciding where to fight
Deny, deny, denial”
There are also many letter “A” gave a sense of someone is screaming
through the process of embracing his or her true self.
As we know Polarization is a process that causes sunglasses to only accept lightwaves
that are coming straight towards the glasses, reducing glare from other reflective surfaces.
Polarized sunglasses helps the wearer to see exactly what they are looking at
more clearly than usual for that reason I created a glasses made by two letters “P”.
Finally, you can read the whole word Polarize and the word "find me"
as a hint for the link to the second page with a little bit of creativity and humor.

My goal is to create a as much Dada feeling as possible
with a touch of bold elements as brushes, illustrations,… and experiement with typos
since Dada, itself is regconizable by uncoventional typographic design,
spontaneous mixing fonts not in any particular orders ,
printing both horizontally and vetically on a single sheet,
and sprinkling texts with randomly chosen symbols such as a pointing hands, a scissors,… .
